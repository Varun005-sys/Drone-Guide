## **Description**
The **Drone Guide Website** is an educational platform for learning about drone components and their usage. It is designed for beginners, students, and hobbyists who want to understand drone technology and safely operate drones.

The website includes:
- Detailed descriptions of **20 essential drone components**
- High-quality images for visual learning
- Step-by-step usage instructions and safety tips
- Responsive and professional layout for desktop and mobile devices
- Sections prepared for tutorials and educational resources

## **Equipment Showcase**

### 1. Drone Frame  
![Drone Frame](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e4/Drone_frame.jpg)  
The structure that holds all drone components together.

### 2. Drone Motors  
![Drone Motors](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/45/Brushless_motor_drone.jpg)  
Spin the propellers to generate thrust and lift.

### 3. Drone Propellers  
![Drone Propellers](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5b/Drone_propeller.jpg)  
Rotating blades that allow the drone to fly.

### 4. Electronic Speed Controller (ESC)  
![ESC](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3f/Drone_ESC.jpg)  
Regulates the speed of the motors by controlling power.

### 5. Flight Controller  
![Flight Controller](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e0/Flight_controller.jpg)  
The brain of the drone that stabilizes and controls flight.

### 6. Li-Po Battery  
![Li-Po Battery](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3a/Lipo_battery.jpg)  
Provides power to the drone and all components.

### 7. GPS Module  
![GPS Module](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/84/GPS_module.jpg)  
Helps drones navigate and return home safely.

### 8. Transmitter  
![Transmitter](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f3/Drone_transmitter.jpg)  
The remote controller used by the pilot to fly the drone.

### 9. Receiver  
![Receiver](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/10/Drone_receiver.jpg)  
Receives signals from the transmitter for controlling the drone.

### 10. Drone Camera  
![Drone Camera](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3e/Drone_camera.jpg)  
Captures photos and videos during drone flight.

### 11. Camera Gimbal  
![Camera Gimbal](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/83/Camera_gimbal_drone.jpg)  
Stabilizes the camera to capture smooth video footage.

### 12. Landing Gear  
![Landing Gear](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/15/Drone_landing_gear.jpg)  
Supports the drone when landing or taking off.

### 13. Power Distribution Board  
![Power Distribution Board](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/69/Drone_power_distribution_board.jpg)  
Distributes power from the battery to all drone components.

### 14. FPV Camera  
![FPV Camera](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0e/FPV_camera_drone.jpg)  
Provides first-person view video to the pilot in real-time.

### 15. Video Transmitter  
![Video Transmitter](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/92/Drone_video_transmitter.jpg)  
Sends live video from the drone to the ground station.

### 16. Drone Antenna  
![Antenna](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f7/Drone_antenna.jpg)  
Improves communication between drone and pilot.

### 17. Compass Sensor  
![Compass Sensor](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/24/Drone_compass_sensor.jpg)  
Helps determine the orientation of the drone in flight.

### 18. Barometer Sensor  
![Barometer Sensor](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5f/Drone_barometer_sensor.jpg)  
Measures altitude and helps stabilize drone at a fixed height.

### 19. Obstacle Avoidance Sensor  
![Obstacle Sensor](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/05/Drone_obstacle_sensor.jpg)  
Detects obstacles and prevents collisions.

### 20. LED Lights  
![LED Lights](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/21/Drone_led_lights.jpg)  
Helps with drone visibility and orientation during flight.
